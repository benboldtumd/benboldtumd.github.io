Ben Boldt's Obsolete Macintosh Collection

Why do I collect Macintosh computers?
When I was in grade school, I really liked computers. In about fourth grade or so, I used a Macintosh for the first time and I knew, right then, that I HAD to have one of those. They were so much more efficient and easier to use. I liked how you could point and click. It took until I was in eighth grade before I had a real Macintosh of my own. It was about five years old and was awarded to me for good grades.

I grew such an attachment to these archaic machines in my childhood that I believe they became a small part of my identity. I lived and breathed Macintosh. So one day I saw several of them sitting, waiting to be recycled. I decided that I would start a Macintosh museum, right in my own home. So here I am, most of the way there.

I look at this collection and I always wonder how I figured out what I love to do at such a young age.
